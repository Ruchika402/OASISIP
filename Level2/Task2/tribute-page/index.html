Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902)

Biography

Swami Vivekananda was one of the greatest spiritual leaders of India. He was born on January 12, 1863 in Kolkata as Narendranath Datta. From a young age he was very intelligent and curious about religion, philosophy, and spirituality.

He later became a disciple of the great saint Ramakrishna Paramahamsa. After the death of his guru, Vivekananda dedicated his life to spreading the teachings of Vedanta and promoting spiritual awareness among people.

In 1893 he delivered a historic speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago. His speech began with the famous words "Sisters and Brothers of America", which received a standing ovation and made him famous around the world.

Swami Vivekananda believed that youth have the power to transform society. He encouraged people to develop self-confidence, strength, and dedication towards serving humanity.

He founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897, an organization that works for education, social service, and spiritual development. Even today his teachings continue to inspire millions of people around the world.

"Arise, awake and stop not till the goal is reached."
